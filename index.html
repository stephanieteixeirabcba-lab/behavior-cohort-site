Who this course is for

Three paths, one course.

This course serves three groups of learners. All are welcome regardless of prior certification status.

Path 1

Preparing for the IBT exam

This course provides the 40-hour training requirement for the International Behavior Therapist (IBT) certification through the IBAO. After completing the course, you may sit for the IBT exam. You will still need to complete 300 hours of supervised practice and other requirements outlined by the IBAO.

Path 2

Deepening your current practice

Already working as a Behaviour Therapist (BT) or in a related role? This course strengthens your skills in neurodiversity-affirming, trauma-informed, and assent-based practice — regardless of whether you plan to sit for any exam.

Path 3

Starting your journey

Interested in becoming a Behaviour Therapist but not sure about certification yet? This course gives you a rigorous foundation in applied behavior analysis from a neurodiversity-affirming lens, so you can decide your next steps from a place of knowledge.

Our commitments

Six non-negotiables.

Everything in this course rests on commitments drawn from peer-reviewed literature and autistic-led scholarship:

  1. Autistic and neurodivergent clients are the experts on their own experience.
  2. Stimming, special interests, and neurodivergent communication are not targets for reduction.
  3. Masking is not a successful outcome.
  4. Assent is ongoing and can be withdrawn — including by clients who don't speak.
  5. Aversives, planned ignoring of distress, and compliance-as-a-goal are not defensible.
  6. Trauma is assumed, not required.

About the IBAO credential

This course is provided by an approved content provider of the International Behavior Analysis Organization (IBAO). The 40 hours of training count toward the educational requirement for the International Behavior Therapist (IBT) certification. The IBT is a globally recognized credential for front-line behaviour therapists delivering ABA services under the supervision of a qualified Behavior Analyst (IBA, QBA, BCBA, or equivalent).

A note on confidentiality

This site and our discussion space are not secure platforms. Never share identifying client information — no names, dates of birth, exact locations, or other identifying details. De-identify all case material thoroughly before posting or submitting. If in doubt, consult your supervising Behavior Analyst.
