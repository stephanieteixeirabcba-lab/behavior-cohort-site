What this cohort is

Practice deepening, not credentialing.

This is a professional-development cohort for Behaviour Therapists (BTs) who are already implementing ABA under the supervision of a credentialed Behavior Analyst (BA) — whether that's a BCBA, IBA, QBA, or other regionally recognized credential. It is not a credentialing pathway and does not replace your jurisdiction's required training, competency assessment, or ongoing supervision. What it does is deepen your practice in the areas the field has moved on: neurodiversity-affirming care, trauma-informed practice, ongoing assent, and respectful work with autistic clients and their families.

We read what autistic self-advocates and autistic-led researchers have written about ABA. We take those critiques seriously. And we learn to do better.

What to expect

Structured learning, unhurried practice.

04

A private discussion space for questions between meetings — threaded, thoughtful, searchable.

Our commitments

Six non-negotiables.

Everything in this cohort rests on commitments drawn from peer-reviewed literature and autistic-led scholarship:

  1. Autistic and neurodivergent clients are the experts on their own experience.
  2. Stimming, special interests, and neurodivergent communication are not targets for reduction.
  3. Masking is not a successful outcome.
  4. Assent is ongoing and can be withdrawn — including by clients who don't speak.
  5. Aversives, planned ignoring of distress, and compliance-as-a-goal are not defensible.
  6. Trauma is assumed, not required.

A note on confidentiality

This site and our discussion space are not secure platforms. Never share identifying client information — no names, dates of birth, exact locations, or other identifying details. De-identify all case material thoroughly before posting or submitting. If in doubt, consult your supervising Behavior Analyst.
