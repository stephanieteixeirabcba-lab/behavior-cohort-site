Autistic-led sources & self-advocacy

These voices are not optional reading. If our practice is to center autistic clients, we must learn from autistic writers and researchers directly — not only through second-hand summaries.

  • Autistic Self Advocacy Network (ASAN). For Whose Benefit? Evidence, Ethics, and Effectiveness of Autism Interventions.
    White paper from the leading autistic self-advocacy organization in the US.
    Autistic-led
  • McGill, O., & Robinson, A. (2020). "Recalling hidden harms": autistic experiences of childhood applied behavioural analysis (ABA).
    Advances in Autism. First-person accounts from autistic adults who received ABA as children.
    Autistic voices
  • Ne'eman, A., & Pellicano, E. (2022). Neurodiversity as politics.
    Ne'eman co-founded ASAN. Foundational framing of the neurodiversity movement.
    Autistic-led
  • Kapp, S. K. (Ed.) (2020). Autistic Community and the Neurodiversity Movement: Stories from the Frontline.
    Open-access book. Essays by autistic advocates, researchers, and community members.
    Book · Autistic-led
  • Thinking Autism Guide & Neuroclastic
    Autistic-run blogs and publications. Ongoing commentary on autism research and practice.
    Autistic-led blogs

A note on jurisdiction

BACB standards govern certification but national and local regulation varies. If you practice outside the United States, check your country's and your employer's requirements. In some jurisdictions RBT-equivalent roles are regulated differently or not at all — supervision and scope standards from your licensing body, not ours, take precedence.

Ground rules

No identifying client information, ever. De-identify thoroughly. Do not share details of a specific behavior plan — that is a conversation for your supervising BCBA. Treat everything shared in this space as confidential within the cohort.
